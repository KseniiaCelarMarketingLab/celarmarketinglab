Common questions
Does this replace legal advice?
No — this is a marketing and localisation checklist, not legal counsel. It flags what to check and why it matters commercially, not a substitute for a lawyer or accountant. VAT rates, consumer-rights wording and mandatory disclosures should always be confirmed with a qualified professional in each market before you launch.
How does the business-type personalisation work?
Three short questions — what you sell, who you sell to, and your team size — adjust which checks appear. A SaaS business sees VAT reverse-charge and data-processing guidance instead of local checkout methods; a physical-goods retailer sees the reverse. Legal and language checks that apply to everyone always stay visible. You can skip this and see every check for every business type at any time.
Do I need to complete every item for every market?
No. Select only the markets you're actually entering. Some checks apply everywhere in the EU (GDPR, the 14-day right of withdrawal, the €10,000 OSS threshold); others are specific to one country — the tool separates the two clearly, tagged by market.
Should I launch all my markets at once?
Usually not. The most common pattern among successful non-EU (and non-local) entrants is to prove the model in one market, fix what breaks, then adapt and sequence the rest — rather than translating everything at once and finding out what's wrong in ten places simultaneously.
Does my progress save?
Yes — your market selection and ticked items are saved in this browser automatically, so you can come back and pick up where you left off.
